Mathematical Foundations of the Hybrid Quantum-Safe Platform

Technical Architecture & Cryptographic Specification

1. Hybrid Key Exchange Architecture

The platform implements a dual-layered cryptographic paradigm, intertwining the Elliptic Curve Diffie-Hellman protocol with a lattice-based Key Encapsulation Mechanism. The orchestration of these discrete primitives produces a unified symmetric session key.

2. X25519 Mathematical Foundation

The classical foundation of the hybrid suite operates over Curve25519, a Montgomery elliptic curve defined over the prime field $\mathbb{F}_p$. The security rests upon the Elliptic Curve Discrete Logarithm Problem (ECDLP).

The Curve25519 equation is defined as:

$$ v^2 = u^3 + 486662u^2 + u \pmod p $$

where the prime $p$ is uniquely specified as $p = 2^{255} - 19$.

In the Diffie-Hellman key agreement, a well-defined base point $G$ generates a prime-order subgroup. Operations utilize scalar multiplication. Let $a$ and $b$ represent the ephemeral private scalars for Alice and Bob, respectively.

$$ A = aG $$ $$ B = bG $$

The shared symmetric secret $S$ is derived commutatively:

$$ S = aB = a(bG) = b(aG) = bA $$

3. ML-KEM-768 Mathematics

The post-quantum mechanism, ML-KEM-768, derives its security bounds from the Module Learning with Errors (M-LWE) problem. In this paradigm, discovering a secret vector in the presence of noise is computationally intractable, effectively resisting Shor's algorithm.

The fundamental M-LWE relation generating the public key $\mathbf{t}$ is:

$$ \mathbf{A} \cdot \mathbf{s} + \mathbf{e} = \mathbf{t} \pmod q $$

Where the components are defined as:

  • $\mathbf{A} \in R_q^{k \times k}$: A uniformly random matrix of polynomials.
  • $\mathbf{s} \in R_q^k$: The secret key vector, with coefficients sampled from a centered binomial distribution $B_\eta$.
  • $\mathbf{e} \in R_q^k$: An error vector, similarly sampled from $B_\eta$, critical for the hardness of the problem.

The addition of the small error $\mathbf{e}$ forces the system into an approximate linear state. Gaussian elimination yields exponentially vast solution spaces, securing $\mathbf{s}$.

4. Polynomial Ring Operations

The mathematical structure underpinning the M-LWE vectors is the polynomial quotient ring:

$$ R_q = \mathbb{Z}_q[x]/(x^n+1) $$

where $n = 256$ is the degree polynomial, and $q = 3329$ is the prime modulus. Computations within this ring involve strict modular reductions at two discrete levels.

Multiplication Process:

  1. Multiply standard polynomials: $C(x) = A(x) \cdot B(x)$
  2. Degree Reduction: Apply the rule $x^n \equiv -1 \pmod{x^n+1}$. Any term $c_k x^k$ where $k \ge 256$ is mapped to $-c_k x^{k-256}$.
  3. Coefficient Reduction: For every coefficient $c_i$, compute $c_i \pmod q$.
Step-by-step example (for n=4, q=17): A(x) = 2x³ + 4x + 1 B(x) = 3x² + 5 1. Standard Multiplication: C(x) = A(x) * B(x) = 6x⁵ + 10x³ + 12x³ + 20x + 3x² + 5 = 6x⁵ + 22x³ + 3x² + 20x + 5 2. Degree Reduction (x⁴ ≡ -1): 6x⁵ = 6x(x⁴) ≡ -6x C'(x) = 22x³ + 3x² + (20x - 6x) + 5 = 22x³ + 3x² + 14x + 5 3. Coefficient Reduction (mod 17): 22 ≡ 5 (mod 17) C_final(x) = 5x³ + 3x² + 14x + 5

5. Number Theoretic Transform (NTT) Acceleration

Naive polynomial multiplication in $R_q$ requires $\mathcal{O}(n^2)$ coefficient multiplications. ML-KEM enforces the use of the Number Theoretic Transform (a discrete Fourier transform analog over a finite field) to map polynomials to the NTT domain.

$$ \text{NTT}(A \cdot B) = \text{NTT}(A) \circ \text{NTT}(B) $$

Pointwise multiplication in the NTT domain reduces the computational complexity to $\mathcal{O}(n \log n)$. Because $q = 3329 \equiv 1 \pmod{256}$, the field contains the necessary $256^{th}$ primitive roots of unity $\zeta$.

6. ML-KEM Encapsulation & Decapsulation

The core KEM functionality requires asymmetric generation and symmetric extraction. The mathematical formulations are defined as follows:

Encapsulation (Sender)

The sender generates a uniform random message $m \in \{0,1\}^{256}$ and an ephemeral secret vector $\mathbf{r}$, alongside error vectors $\mathbf{e_1}$ and $e_2$. The ciphertext $c = (\mathbf{u}, v)$ is computed over the public key $\mathbf{t}$:

$$ \mathbf{u} = \text{NTT}^{-1}(\text{NTT}(\mathbf{A}^T) \circ \text{NTT}(\mathbf{r})) + \mathbf{e_1} $$ $$ v = \text{NTT}^{-1}(\text{NTT}(\mathbf{t}^T) \circ \text{NTT}(\mathbf{r})) + e_2 + \lfloor \frac{q}{2} \rceil \cdot m $$

Decapsulation (Receiver)

The receiver utilizes the private key $\mathbf{s}$ to strip the public matrix parameters, extracting the noisy message, which is then rounded to recover $m$:

$$ m' = \text{Compress}(v - \mathbf{s}^T \mathbf{u}) $$

Proof of correctness:

$ v - \mathbf{s}^T \mathbf{u} $
$ = (\mathbf{t}^T\mathbf{r} + e_2 + \lfloor \frac{q}{2} \rceil m) - \mathbf{s}^T(\mathbf{A}^T\mathbf{r} + \mathbf{e_1}) $
$ = ((\mathbf{A}\mathbf{s} + \mathbf{e})^T\mathbf{r} + e_2 + \lfloor \frac{q}{2} \rceil m) - (\mathbf{s}^T\mathbf{A}^T\mathbf{r} + \mathbf{s}^T\mathbf{e_1}) $
$ = \mathbf{s}^T\mathbf{A}^T\mathbf{r} + \mathbf{e}^T\mathbf{r} + e_2 + \lfloor \frac{q}{2} \rceil m - \mathbf{s}^T\mathbf{A}^T\mathbf{r} - \mathbf{s}^T\mathbf{e_1} $
$ = \lfloor \frac{q}{2} \rceil m + (\mathbf{e}^T\mathbf{r} + e_2 - \mathbf{s}^T\mathbf{e_1}) $

Because the term $(\mathbf{e}^T\mathbf{r} + e_2 - \mathbf{s}^T\mathbf{e_1})$ consists entirely of small error coefficients, it is vastly smaller than $\lfloor q/2 \rceil$. The compression/rounding function safely discards this noise, leaving the exact bitstring $m$.

$$ S = S_{ECC} \parallel S_{PQC} $$ $$ K = \text{HKDF}(S) $$

8. HKDF Mathematics

The HMAC-based Extract-and-Expand Key Derivation Function (RFC 5869) rigorously processes the concatenated input key material (IKM).

Extract Phase: Homogenizes the dispersion of entropy from the inputs to produce a fixed-length pseudorandom key (PRK).

$$ \text{PRK} = \text{HMAC-SHA256}(\text{Salt}, \text{IKM}) $$

Expand Phase: Deterministically expands the PRK into the cryptographically robust Output Keying Material (OKM) mapping to the 256-bit space required by AES.

$$ \text{OKM} = \text{HMAC-SHA256}(\text{PRK}, \text{Info} \parallel \text{0x01}) $$

9. AES-256-GCM Mathematics

The final communication streams are encrypted utilizing the derived session key via AES-256 operating in Galois/Counter Mode (GCM). This provides Authenticated Encryption with Associated Data (AEAD).

Confidentiality (Counter Mode):

A 96-bit Initialization Vector (IV) initializes a counter. Successive counter blocks are encrypted and XORed with the plaintext.

$$ C_i = P_i \oplus E_K(IV \parallel \text{counter}_i) $$

Integrity & Authentication (GHASH):

A hash subkey $H = E_K(0^{128})$ defines an evaluation point over the Galois field $\text{GF}(2^{128})$. A polynomial is constructed where the coefficients are the ciphertext blocks and associated data, generating the authentication tag.

$$ \text{Tag} = \text{GHASH}_H(A, C) \oplus E_K(IV \parallel 0^{31}1) $$
